## Draft 1 Body

# 70 AI Prompt Examples to Manage Your Restaurant Website With ChatGPT

Restaurant websites usually fall behind in small, ordinary ways: a menu price changes, a photo is out of date, a review needs a reply, a new branch opens, or a special gets posted everywhere except the website.

KrabiClaw lets restaurant owners manage those jobs from ChatGPT. You can type a normal request, review what ChatGPT proposes, and let it update the right part of your KrabiClaw site. The best prompts are specific enough to act on, but they do not need to sound technical.

Use these examples as starting points. Replace the names, dishes, neighborhoods, and details with your own.

## Start or Finish a Restaurant Website From Google Maps

If you are setting up a new restaurant site, start with the information you already have: your Google Maps listing, business name, contact details, address, hours, and a few photos.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Set up my restaurant website from this Google Maps listing, then show me what details you found before creating anything."
- "Use these business details to create a new restaurant website: name, address, phone, email, opening hours, cuisine, and short description."
- "Create the first version of my restaurant site, but leave anything uncertain as a question for me."
- "Show me a preview of the site before it goes live."
- "Check what is still missing from my new restaurant website and give me one next step at a time."

## Improve Your Homepage and First Impression

Your homepage should make three things clear fast: what kind of restaurant you are, where you are, and why someone should visit or book.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Rewrite our homepage headline so it sounds warm, local, and clear for first-time guests."
- "Make the homepage subtitle mention that we are near the beach and serve fresh Thai seafood."
- "Review the homepage and tell me the highest-impact change to make first."
- "Show me which homepage fields I can edit, then suggest better wording for the weak ones."
- "Remove the outdated sentence about our old opening hours from the homepage."
- "Make our call to action more direct without sounding pushy."

## Tell a Better About Page and Brand Story

An About page helps guests trust the restaurant before they visit. The prompt should give ChatGPT real material: who started it, what the kitchen cares about, and what makes the place specific.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Rewrite our About page using this story: we are a family-run restaurant, we buy seafood locally, and our recipes come from southern Thailand."
- "Make this About page less generic and more specific to our neighborhood and team."
- "Shorten our brand story so it works on mobile but still feels personal."
- "Add a friendly paragraph about why guests should book ahead for dinner."
- "Turn these rough notes into website copy for our story section."

## Upload and Place Restaurant Photos

Photos are often the fastest way to make a restaurant site feel current. Tell ChatGPT what the image shows and where you want it used.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "I uploaded a new dining room photo. Make it the homepage image and write clear alt text for it."
- "Use this seafood platter photo for the homepage story section."
- "Find our uploaded food photos and suggest which one should be the main hero image."
- "Use this logo file as our site logo."
- "Update the alt text for our gallery photos so they describe the dish or room accurately."
- "Use this photo for the Ao Nang location page."

## Build a Menu From Scratch

A good menu prompt includes sections, item names, descriptions, prices, currency, and any labels you care about.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Create a dinner menu with these sections: Small Plates, Seafood, Curries, Noodles, Desserts, and Drinks."
- "Add these five lunch specials to our menu and group them under Lunch."
- "Set our menu prices in Thai baht."
- "Create a drinks menu with cocktails, beer, wine, soft drinks, and fresh juices."
- "Add short descriptions to these dishes, but keep them simple enough for tourists to understand."
- "Create a vegetarian section from these items and keep the original items in their main sections too."

## Import a Menu From a Photo or PDF

If your printed menu already exists, upload a photo or PDF and ask ChatGPT to extract it. This is useful, but always review prices, spelling, and section names before publishing.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Import our printed menu from this photo and group the dishes into Starters, Mains, Desserts, and Drinks."
- "Extract the menu items from this PDF, keep the prices, and flag anything you are unsure about."
- "Turn this menu photo into a draft menu and ask me before deleting or replacing anything."
- "Import this Thai and English menu, then show me the items that need review."
- "Use this menu scan to create a website menu, but do not publish it until I approve the sections."

## Edit Menu Items, Sections, and Order

Small menu edits are exactly the kind of work that should not require opening a CMS and hunting for the right screen.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Move our best-selling curry to the top of the Mains section."
- "Rename Starters to Small Plates across the menu."
- "Add allergen notes to the dishes where I mention peanuts, gluten, or shellfish."
- "Update the price of the grilled prawns to 320 baht."
- "Delete the old mango cheesecake item from Desserts."
- "Reorder the Drinks section so cocktails appear before soft drinks."
- "Add this dish photo to the pad thai menu item."

## Manage Locations, Hours, and Branch Details

Restaurants with more than one branch need location prompts that name the branch clearly. If the account has multiple sites or locations, ask ChatGPT to confirm the one it will update.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Add our second location and copy the menu from our first location."
- "Update the phone number and opening hours for the Ao Nang location."
- "Change the email address for our beachfront branch."
- "Copy the photos and Q&A from our main location to our new branch."
- "Show me all locations and ask which one I want to update."
- "Use this new exterior photo as the location page image."

## Create Posts, Offers, and Announcements

Short posts are for timely updates, offers, events, and announcements. Keep evergreen examples flexible so they do not become stale.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Write a short announcement that our new tasting menu is live and publish it on our website."
- "Turn this text into a simple offer post with a clear call to action."
- "Create a post about our private dining option and keep the tone friendly."
- "Use this food photo as the image for the announcement."
- "Post this update on our website and Facebook page, if Facebook is connected."
- "Schedule this announcement instead of publishing it immediately."

## Draft Restaurant Blog Posts for SEO

Restaurant blog posts are useful when they answer questions guests already search for: what to order, what to expect, where to eat, how to book, and what makes the area special.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Draft a blog post about what first-time guests should know before booking dinner with us."
- "Write an article about our most popular southern Thai dishes and include an FAQ section."
- "Create a draft blog post about where to eat seafood near our neighborhood."
- "Add an SEO description for this article before I publish it."
- "Suggest five blog post ideas based on our menu, location, and customer questions."
- "Set this uploaded restaurant photo as the featured image for the blog post."

## Reply to Reviews and Keep Q&A Useful

KrabiClaw can help you review Q&A and write owner replies. It should not claim to delete bad reviews or change ratings. Use it to respond well and keep guest-facing answers accurate.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Show me recent reviews that still need a reply."
- "Write a friendly reply to this five-star review and mention that we are glad they liked the seafood."
- "Draft a calm reply to this critical review and thank the guest for the feedback."
- "Add a Q&A answer about parking near the restaurant."
- "Add a Q&A answer about vegetarian and gluten-free options."
- "Reorder our Q&A so booking, parking, and dietary questions appear first."

## Triage Contact Messages and Reservation Requests

You may not want ChatGPT to answer guests automatically, but it can help you see what needs attention and decide what to handle first.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Show me reservation requests that still need attention."
- "Summarize new contact messages from the last week and group them by urgency."
- "Which inquiries mention private dining or large groups?"
- "Find messages where the guest asked about dietary requirements."
- "Help me triage the inbox and tell me the one request I should handle first."

## Use Analytics to Decide What to Improve

Analytics prompts are where ChatGPT becomes more than a writing assistant. Ask what is working, what changed, and which page deserves attention next.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "How many visitors did our site get in the last 30 days?"
- "Which pages got the most visitors last month?"
- "Did traffic go up after we changed the homepage photo?"
- "What menu page gets the most attention?"
- "Which page should we improve first based on traffic and guest intent?"
- "Compare this month with the previous month and explain the change in plain language."

## Ask for More Reservations Without Inventing a Magic Growth Tool

A broad prompt like "help me get more reservations" should lead ChatGPT to read your site analytics, check menu and location completeness, look at reservation demand, and suggest one concrete next move for you to approve. It should not pretend to run ads, change prices automatically, or optimize availability by itself.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Help me get more reservations this month. Check our traffic, menu completeness, and reservation inquiries, then suggest one next step."
- "Business has been slow this week. Look at the website data and tell me what to improve first."
- "If our menu page is getting traffic but reservations are low, help me improve the call to action."
- "Which branch or menu should we promote right now based on site activity?"
- "I do not know what to do next. Review our site and inbox, then give me the highest-impact task."

## Connect Google Business and Keep Local Details Aligned

Google Business prompts should focus on connection, account selection, location sync, and review visibility. Do not ask ChatGPT to edit arbitrary Google listing fields unless your setup supports that specific action.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Check whether our Google Business Profile is connected."
- "Help me connect our Google Business Profile to this restaurant site."
- "Sync our Google Business location into the website."
- "Show me which Google Business locations are available to connect."
- "Pull in the latest location details where the integration supports it."

## Publish to Facebook and Connected Social Channels

When social publishing is connected and permitted, ChatGPT can help turn a website update into a short post and publish it to the right channels.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Check whether our Facebook page is connected."
- "Write a short Facebook version of this website update."
- "Publish this announcement to our website and Facebook page."
- "Use this uploaded photo for the post before publishing."
- "Turn this offer into a short social post, but keep the website copy more detailed."

## Translate Menus and Website Content

Translation prompts help restaurants serve international visitors. Ask for a scope check first if you are translating a large site or menu.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Translate our menu and homepage into French for visitors."
- "Add German as a site language and estimate what needs translating."
- "Translate this menu item description into English and Thai."
- "Start a translation draft for our main pages, then show me what needs review."
- "Show me translation jobs that are still waiting for review."
- "Publish the approved translations after I review them."

## Launch, Domains, and Final Checks

Before launch, ask ChatGPT to check the site like an owner would: domain, homepage, menu, location, contact details, photos, and calls to action.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Help me connect my custom domain and show me the DNS records I need."
- "Check whether my DNS records are active yet."
- "Make my custom domain the main website address once it is ready."
- "Give me a dashboard link for the domain setup page."
- "Review the site before launch and list only the issues that would stop a guest from booking or visiting."

## Ask KrabiClaw for Human Help

Some jobs are better handled by a person, especially bigger copy refreshes, photo selection, or launch polish. Use a managed-service request when you want the KrabiClaw team to help.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Create a work request asking the KrabiClaw team to polish our homepage copy."
- "Create a work request to review our menu photos and choose the best ones for the site."
- "Show me my open managed-service requests."
- "Ask the KrabiClaw team to help us prepare the website before launch."

# 60 AI Prompt Examples to Manage Your Tour or Experience Business With ChatGPT

Tour, workshop, class, and activity websites need more than attractive copy. Guests need to understand what happens, how long it takes, where to meet, what is included, what to bring, whether it is suitable for them, and how to book.

KrabiClaw lets experience operators manage those details through ChatGPT. You can create and edit experience listings, place photos and videos, review bookings, answer Q&A, look at traffic, publish updates, and keep launch details organized without turning every small change into a dashboard session.

Use these prompts as copy-paste starting points. Replace the examples with your own tour, class, workshop, or activity details.

## Start a Site From Google Maps or Business Details

If you already have a Google Maps listing, use it as the starting point. If not, give ChatGPT your business name, location, contact details, and the kinds of experiences you offer.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Set up my experience website from this Google Maps listing and show me what details you found before creating anything."
- "Create a new workshop website using these business details, but ask me before guessing missing information."
- "Create the first version of my tour business site and show me a preview before it goes live."
- "Check what is missing from my new experience website and guide me through one setup task at a time."
- "Use these uploaded photos as starter media for the site."

## Create a New Experience Listing

A strong listing prompt includes the title, short tagline, description, highlights, duration, price, capacity, schedule, meeting point, what is included, what guests should bring, and cancellation policy.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Create a pottery workshop listing with a two-hour duration, eight-person capacity, and this meeting point."
- "Create a longtail boat tour listing from these notes and include highlights, what is included, and what guests should bring."
- "Add a new cooking class with this price, duration, schedule, and maximum group size."
- "Create a beginner-friendly yoga class listing and make the description clear for first-time guests."
- "Draft the listing first and ask me before publishing it."
- "Make this experience active only after I approve the final copy."

## Improve Existing Experience Pages

Guests often hesitate when a listing is vague. Ask ChatGPT to make the practical details clearer, not just prettier.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Rewrite this tour description so guests understand what is included and what they should bring."
- "Make the highlights easier to scan on mobile."
- "Add a cancellation policy to this experience."
- "Update the meeting point and make it clear when guests should arrive."
- "Rewrite the tagline so it explains the value in one sentence."
- "Check this experience page for missing booking information."
- "Make the description more reassuring for beginners without overpromising."

## Manage Duration, Price, Capacity, and Schedule

Booking pages need accurate structured details. These prompts are useful when operations change but the experience itself stays the same.

Copy-paste prompts:

- "Update this workshop to a three-hour duration and a maximum of six guests."
- "Change the price of the island tour to 1,800 baht per person."
- "Add the weekly schedule from these notes."
- "Update the included items to mention equipment, drinking water, and pickup."
- "Add 'bring sunscreen, swimwear, and a dry bag' to the what-to-bring section."
- "Update the cancellation policy to say guests can cancel up to 24 hours before the start time."
